Get Started

To get started, press the Start button and answer four questions to find your way to school on time:

1. What is the name of your school? Start typing in any part of your school's name and you'll see matching names names appear as you type. Continue typing or select a name in the drop-down. Once a school name is entered, the school's office phone number will appear. Call this number to report an absence. On an iPhone or Android smartphone, click on the number to call. Click Next to move to the next question.

2. When do you want to arrive? Choose either Today or Tomorrow, then choose Start of School or End of School. You may also enter your own time. Click Next to move on.

3. How are you getting there? Select Walking, CTA/Metra, or Driving. Click Next.

4. Where are you? Enter the address of the location where you are starting from. If you are in Chicago, you may just enter the street address (example: 1 W Madison). If you are starting outside of Chicago, you must also enter the city and state. If you are on a GPS capable device, you may use the Find Me link to automatically fill in this information. Click the Next button to move on.

Once you answer all four questions, you will see a summary of your choices. Use the Edit buttons to go back and change your choices. On iPhone and Android devices, click on the Call button to call the school office. You might need to report an absence.

Press the Go! button to get directions to school and a recommended time to leave. The route recommendation and dearture time estimate are automatically generated. While they are good enough for average conditions, take care to make sure the recommendations are right for you. Factors such as neighborhood conditions, traffic, or walking pace may differ daily and by location. To help, street traffic congestion for Chicago are indicated on the map if you choose Driving or CTA/Metra for your travel mode. Green is light traffic, yellow is moderate traffic, and red is heavy traffic conditions.

Coming Back for a Visit

Go To School saves your information in your device. Read more about local web storage here. When you come back with the same computer, tablet, or smartphone, you will not have to reenter anything. Go To School will automatically send you right to the summary page. Just click Go to see you directions.

About

This app was the brainchild of Tom Kompare wondering what time he had to get to his CTA Red Line stop in order to pick up his daughter from kindergarten on time. So, Tom really built this web application for himself, but he thought he ought to share.

Tom believes in sharing so strongly, that the code behind this site is freely available to anyone who may want to implement this in their school district. You may find the code here.
